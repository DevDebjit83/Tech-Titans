Self-defense is a multifaceted skill set encompassing more than just physical techniques; it includes situational awareness, assertiveness, and de-escalation strategies. Beyond the physical aspect, self-defense training can enhance confidence and mental well-being, enabling individuals to recognize and avoid potential threats. Effective self-defense focuses on leveraging the body's natural strengths and targeting an attacker's vulnerabilities. It's not limited to physical attacks but also extends to verbal, emotional, and psychological threats. Regular practice is essential to maintain proficiency and muscle memory. Self-defense is considered a fundamental human right and varies based on age, physical abilities, and the specific threat. It emphasizes using force proportionate to the danger and seeking help from authorities when necessary. Ultimately, self-defense is about empowerment, enabling individuals to take control of their safety and well-being.
